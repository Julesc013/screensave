## Purpose

`PX32` lands the next bounded set of experimental field families for Plasma without turning the product into a scenic, ecological, or world-simulated renderer.

## Why PX32 Exists Now

- `PX20` made the authored and validation substrate real enough for later-wave truth.
- `PX30` made bounded non-raster output and treatment seams real.
- `PX31` added the first bounded new field-family slice.
- `PX32` uses those seams to deepen Plasma with a later bounded field-family subset rather than widening stable scope.

## Relationship To PX31 And Later Wave 4 Work

- `PX31` remains the first new field-family tranche for chemical, lattice, and caustic studies.
- `PX32` adds the next bounded family slice and the first bounded dimensional uplift beyond `heightfield`.
- Later Wave 4 work may build on these seams, but it should not have to invent the field-family-II wiring or the non-flat presentation subset again.

## Actual Field-Families-II Capabilities Landed

The implemented bounded subset is:

- `aurora_curtain_ribbon`
- `substrate_vein_coral`
- `arc_discharge`

Those surfaces are now:

- compiled into the runtime plan from a bounded experimental preset slice
- validated explicitly instead of remaining taxonomy only
- visible in BenchLab snapshot and report truth
- carried through the existing output, treatment, and presentation seams without a content-system redesign

## Which Target Families Were Actually Implemented

Implemented in this tranche:

- stronger aurora or curtain-ribbon field behavior through `PLASMA_EFFECT_AURORA`
- bounded substrate, vein, or coral-growth abstraction through `PLASMA_EFFECT_SUBSTRATE`
- bounded arc, filament, or discharge abstraction through `PLASMA_EFFECT_ARC`

Not implemented in this tranche:

- scenic aurora sky presentation
- literal coral, habitat, or biology simulation
- object-demo electricity or machinery framing
- broader ecological or world-simulated behavior

## How They Integrate With Existing Outputs And Treatments

The current bounded preset slice uses the new families compositionally:

- `aurora_curtain` keeps raster plus `glow_edge` and uses bounded `curtain` presentation
- `ribbon_aurora` uses banded output plus `accent_pass` and bounded `ribbon` presentation
- `substrate_relief` uses raster plus `emboss_edge` and `phosphor` with bounded `bounded_surface` presentation
- `filament_extrusion` uses contour plus `glow_edge` and `accent_pass` with bounded `contour_extrusion` presentation

This keeps the new families tied to existing Plasma grammar rather than inventing a separate visual stack.

## Settings And BenchLab Truth For The Supported Subset

`PX32` does not add a new dedicated field-family-II UI.
The supported subset remains preset-driven and experimental-first:

- stable defaults remain the preserved classic raster-plus-flat path
- the new field-family-II surfaces live in the bounded experimental preset slice
- the existing `content_filter` setting remains the truthful gate for reaching that slice
- the existing `effect_mode` setting now accepts the bounded `aurora_curtain_ribbon`, `substrate_vein_coral`, and `arc_discharge` family vocabulary

BenchLab now reports the supported subset through:

- `generator_family`
- `output_family`
- `output_mode`
- treatment-slot fields
- `presentation_mode`
- requested, resolved, and degraded lane truth

## Degrade Policy For The Supported Subset

- The new field-family-II slice is truthful only on the bounded implemented subset.
- Non-flat presentation requests degrade honestly back to `flat` when premium presentation cannot be honored.
- Unsupported broader field-family-II aspirations remain explicit non-claims rather than silent fallback.

## What Remains Bounded Or Unsupported

- no scenic aurora sky behavior
- no literal habitat, ecology, or coral simulation
- no object-demo discharge identity
- no exhaustive lane proof across every new field family
- no Wave 4 authoring, community, provenance, or integration work

## Scope Boundary

`PX32` covers only the later bounded set of new field families.
It remains field-derived, abstract, bounded, and experimental-first.

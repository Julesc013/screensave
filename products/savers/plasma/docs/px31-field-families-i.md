# PX31 Field Families I

## Purpose

`PX31` lands the first bounded new field-family set for Plasma after the preserved classic families.
It widens the field language in the safest next order without drifting into field-families II or broader dimensional work.

## Actual Field-Families-I Capabilities Landed

The implemented subset is:

- `chemical_cellular_growth`
- `lattice_quasi_crystal`
- `caustic_marbling`

This subset is real through bounded experimental presets, runtime simulation branches, validation notes, and BenchLab generator-family truth.

## Which Priority Families Were Actually Implemented

All three priority families in this tranche are present as bounded studies:

- chemical or cellular growth
- lattice or quasi-crystal
- caustic or marbling

They are intentionally abstract and field-derived.
They do not claim literal ecology, weather, scenery, or broader world simulation.

## How They Integrate With Existing Outputs And Treatments

The new field-family subset stays compositional with the existing runtime grammar:

- `ascii_reactor` couples `chemical_cellular_growth` with `ascii_glyph`
- `matrix_lattice` couples `lattice_quasi_crystal` with `matrix_glyph`
- `cellular_bloom` couples `chemical_cellular_growth` with `raster` plus `glow_edge`
- `quasi_crystal_bands` couples `lattice_quasi_crystal` with `posterized_bands` plus `accent_pass`
- `caustic_waterlight` couples `caustic_marbling` with `contour_bands` plus `glow_edge`

That keeps the new generators inside the current output and treatment grammar rather than inventing a separate subsystem.

## Settings And BenchLab Truth

`PX31` keeps settings placement bounded:

- no new field-family UI surface is introduced
- the existing `effect_mode` control now includes the bounded experimental field-family-I values
- the stable default path still resolves to the classic families unless the user or preset opts into the experimental slice

BenchLab now reports the supported subset through:

- `generator_family`
- `output_family`
- `output_mode`
- existing treatment-slot fields and `profile_class`

## Degrade Policy For The Supported Subset

- The field-family-I subset is admitted only through bounded experimental presets.
- The subset remains lower-band-safe for the admitted presets and does not depend on new dimensional work.
- Unsupported lanes, broader family combinations, and later-wave breadth are still kept explicit rather than hidden behind fallback wording.

## What Remains Bounded Or Unsupported

- no field-families II work
- no new dimensional modes
- no exhaustive generator matrix across every output and treatment
- no scenic or literal interpretation of the new families
- no stable widening by implication

## Scope Boundary

`PX31` covers only the first bounded set of new field families.
It does not claim broader field-family breadth, broader dimensional work, or a later-wave recut of the stable product.
